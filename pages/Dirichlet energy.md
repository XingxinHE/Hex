# 📝Definition
**📄Definition 1**
Given
- an open set  $\Omega\subseteq \mathbb{R}^n$
- a function $u:\Omega\to\mathbb{R}$ 
The Dirichlet energy of the function $u$ is a [[real number]]:
$$
{\displaystyle E[u]={\frac {1}{2}}\int _{\Omega }\|\nabla u(x)\|^{2}\,dx,}
$$
- where $\nabla u:\Omega\to\mathbb{R}^n$ denotes the [[gradient]] [[vector field]] of the function $u$. 
___
**📃Definition 2**
Let $h$ be a real-valued [[harmonic function]] on a bounded domain $\Omega$, then the Dirichlet energy is defined as
$$
\int_\Omega|\nabla h|^2dx,
$$
where $\nabla$  is the gradient.


# 🧠Intuition
The Dirichlet energy is a measure of how variable a function is.
